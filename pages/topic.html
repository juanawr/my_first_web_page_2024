Generative AI in Video Games

Introduction

Video games have evolved significantly in recent years, influencing the design of innovative technologies like Generative AI. This technology, which offers challenges, controlled environments, and instant feedback, has become more precise and efficient. Generative AI has become an essential part of the video game industry, enabling developers to create more realistic, immersive, and complex games. While it does not handle the entire development process, its presence is crucial, acting as a co-designer and contributing to the industry's growth and success.

Video games are electronic games where players control images on a video screen, combining chance, competition, and role-playing. The video game industry has evolved significantly since the first console, "The Brown Box," invented in 1967. The development of virtual reality (VR) and artificial intelligence (AI) has significantly influenced the industry. AI is the ability of a digital computer or computer-controlled robot to perform tasks associated with intelligent beings, reducing monotony and eliminating repetitive tasks. In the video game industry, AI plays a dominant role, using artificial neural networks and machine learning techniques. Generative AI, which refers to deep-learning models that generate high-quality content based on trained data, is gaining interest and traction in the industry. As the need for immersive virtuality increases, game makers aim to make games more unpredictable and interactive, using Generative AI to accelerate game production, create better content, and protect intellectual property.
